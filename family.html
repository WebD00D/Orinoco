Our Family

The Orinoco family of cigar lovers.

Orinoco 1613 is a cigar company steeped in history and tradition.  Orinoco is the original American Indian word for tobacco.  Grown by the tribe living in Virginia when the English landed at Jamestown, the tobacco intrigued the English settlers. The tribal chieftain, Chief Powhatan ruled not only Virginia but also parts of Maryland and North Carolina. 

The Chief is best known for his daughter, Pocahontas who married Englishman John Rolfe.  On Bermuda Hundred, their plantation located on the South bank of the James River, the first tobacco exported from the New World to Europe was grown. From the wharf at the plantation the tobacco was loaded abroad the ship, Elizabeth in the year 1613. 

Orinoco 1613 commemorates and celebrates that event.  We make our ultra premium cigars from the finest tobacco grown worldwide while staying true to the tradition and vision of John Rolfe.
​
The main line cigars of Orinoco 1613 are named after the three ships that landed at Jamestown in 1607.
